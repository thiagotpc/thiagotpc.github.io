Bio or Resumè

Thiago has been an Information Technology (IT) and Internet professional since 1999. He knew MS-DOS, mIRC, Netscape, Windows 3.11, Novell Networks, ICQ, ASP and many other technologies!

Currently, he is an IT analyst at UTFPR Londrina in support, maintenance, analysis, and development activities.

He has expertise in web standards (front-end), RESTful services (back-end with PHP), information architecture, usability, and accessibility.

He has interests and researches about Software Engineering, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Information Systems, Information Science, and Data Science.

He has an academic background in Computer Science (bachelor's, master's, and PhD degrees).

Throughout his academic training, he conducted research on different contexts of IT and computing use, such as the impact of e-business on traditional business models, e-commerce development, applications for Digital TV, exploratory search on the Web, documentation and help tools for software users, and usability and user experience evaluation in touchable holographic solutions. His PhD thesis received the national Junia Coutinho Prize for Best Doctoral Thesis on HCI. See more details about his academic trajectory

He was a coordinator and professor of the Specialization in Web Development (post-graduate course). He was also a visiting professor in other post-graduate courses (UEL, UEM, FAP-CE, Pitagoras, Univem, etc.). Furthermore, he teaches HTML, CSS, Javascript, WebAPIs, PHP, Restful, UX, Usability, Accessibility, Information Architecture, etc. For 12 years, he was an entrepreneur in a digital agency.

Occasionally, he contributes to some third-party projects. He is a working group member about Web Accessibility at the W3C Brazil office and contributes to some Ceweb.br projects. He also contributed to the Brazil project e-Gov and was a technical evangelist for Web Accessibility, Web APIs, HTML5, and CSS3! Likewise, he participated in the special committee on the accessibility of the Brazilian Association of Technical Standards (ABNT in Portuguese). He contributed to the creation of NBR 17060:2022 on accessibility requirements for mobile devices and NBR 17725:2025 on web accessibility.
